Merit-Based Incentive Payment System

Modernizing Medicare to provide better care and smarter spending

Overview

About the project

Overview: The Merit-based Incentive Payment System (MIPS) is a new way by which eligible clinicians will be measured by U.S. Dept. of Health Services to determine if they are providing quality patient care in an economically efficient way.
This consolidation will reduce the aggregate level of financial penalties physicians otherwise could have faced, and it also provides a greater potential for bonus payments.

The Solution: A robust & customizable web based application which can record data, provide scorecard and conduct what-if analysis.

Client: Regenstrief Institute, Inc.

Timeline: 4 Months

Team: 2 Designers, 4 Developers

The Challenge

Provide an opportunity to revise, rework and improve the data reporting system using electronic health records to reduce the overall administrative burdens for physicians and advisors.

My Role

I worked as the Team Lead and UX Designer on this project. Some of my responsibilities included:

  1. Planning, defining scope & strategy
  2. Conduct user interviews & observations
  3. Facilitate ideation & design decisions
  4. Sketching, wireframing and prototyping
  5. Front-end dev & collaborate with other devs
  6. User testing and evaluation sessions

The Impact

Currently used by Quality Improvement Advisors (QIA) to guide 15,000+ clinicians across universities, health systems and independent provider-practices in five Midwestern states.

Success Metrics

  1. Should be easy to report & analyze data
  2. Should be customizable according to user needs
  3. Automate and reduce time in editing tables
  4. Increase in revenue by daily active users

Research

Who are the users?

The users of this tool are Quality Improvement Advisors (QIA). QIA's acts as an internal consultant and resource to a health organization with the goal of driving improvement in quality, cost efficiency and patient satisfaction. They gather, compile and analyze clinical reports, identify gaps/strengths, make appropriate recommendations for corrective actions for quality improvements and track progress and results.

Interviews

We interviewed four Quality Improvement Advisors from different health organizations such as IU Health, Purdue Health etc. I studied their current workflow and the technologies used. After conducting the interviews we created user journey maps with problems and opportunities.

Observations

Observations were conducted to get and understanding the QIA's process of data collection from various pysicians and providers. The findings showed that the current process involved a lot of redundant and time consuming tasks / steps which needed to be simplified and worked on.

Research insights

Understanding the user

After conducting interviews and observations I created user personas with information about their behaviors, technologies used, pain points & their needs.

QIAs mainly use MS Excel spreadsheets to enter data and view the final scorecards.

They want to see the results in a visual format which is easy to understand and explain.

Quality Improvement Advisor (QIA)

QIAs frequently edit values in the tables to check the scores and compare results.

QIAs want to quickly find relevant the practises from hundreds of others in the list.

Journey Mapping

Identifying user journey

User Needs

  1. Effectively report data into database
  2. Get analytics and provider score trend
  3. Easy to save reports & edit any changes
  4. Different users have different habits & issues
  5. Save previous data to compare them

Painpoints

  1. Long reports and high error rates
  2. Switching and navigating between reports
  3. Unable to analyze data and what it means
  4. Time consuming MS Excel spreadsheets
  5. Unable to search relevant data / practices

Must Give

  1. A way to organize reports based on providers
  2. Visualization to analyze trends & compare score
  3. An easy to add and search data in tables
  4. A way to check different scores to recommend
  5. Easy to navigate between different reports

Conclusion

Reflection & learnings

This was my first project where I was not only responsible for UX design but also project management, strategy, and team leadership. We faced challenges in each step as the project was very technical and no one had expertise in healthcare domain. I learned that every problem looks difficult initially, but with the right approach and having a design mindset we can solve any issues effectively. As a designer, we should constantly ask questions, challenge our assumptions and be critical about every decision. Designing effectively means understanding the user goals and organization goals because both are needed for a product's success.
